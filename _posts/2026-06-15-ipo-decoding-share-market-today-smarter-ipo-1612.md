## What the Data Actually Says
When evaluating an IPO, it's essential to look at the company's financials, promoters, and use of funds. The IPO pipeline is currently filled with companies from various sectors, including technology, healthcare, and finance. In India, the Securities and Exchange Board of India (SEBI) regulates the IPO process, ensuring that companies provide accurate and transparent information to investors. In the US, the Securities and Exchange Commission (SEC) plays a similar role. To apply for an IPO in India, investors can use the ASBA (Application Supported by Blocked Amount) or UPI (Unified Payments Interface) method, which allows them to block the required amount in their bank account until the allotment is finalized. Indian traders can open a free account at [Zerodha](https://zerodha.com/open-account/) to start investing in IPOs. In the US, investors can apply for IPOs through their brokerage accounts, such as [Webull](https://webull.com/).

## How This Affects Each Country

## Key Numbers to Know
When evaluating an IPO, it's essential to look at the company's valuation, financials, and promoters. The IPO GMP (Grey Market Premium) is also an important indicator, as it reflects the market's expectations of the company's performance. However, it's essential to remember that the IPO GMP is not always a reliable indicator, and investors should do their own research before investing. For example, the Hexagon Nutrition IPO, which was listed recently, had a GMP of around 20-25%, but the actual listing gain was much higher. ## The Risk Nobody's Talking About
One of the significant risks associated with IPO investing is the lack of liquidity. Many IPOs are not heavily traded, which can make it difficult for investors to sell their shares quickly. Additionally, the IPO market is highly volatile, and prices can fluctuate rapidly. It's essential for investors to have a long-term perspective and not to invest more than they can afford to lose. I think it's also important to remember that IPO investing is not a guaranteed-money pitch, and investors should be cautious of hype and rumors. Honestly, I've seen many investors get burned by investing in IPOs without doing their due diligence.
