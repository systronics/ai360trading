This simple yet effective rule allocates 50% of our income towards necessary expenses, 30% towards discretionary spending, and 20% towards saving and debt repayment. For instance, if you're living in the US, earning $50,000 per year, you should aim to allocate $25,000 towards necessary expenses like rent and utilities, $15,000 towards discretionary spending like entertainment and hobbies, and $10,000 towards saving and debt repayment.
