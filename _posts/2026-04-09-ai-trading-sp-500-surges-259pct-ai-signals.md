## Trader FAQs
### What is delta in options trading explained in Hindi?
Delta is a measure of the rate of change of the option price with respect to the underlying stock price. It is expressed as a decimal value between 0 and 1. For example, if the delta value of a call option is 0.55, it means that the option price will increase by 0.55% for every 1% increase in the underlying stock price.
### Theta decay options how much do I lose per day?
Theta decay is the loss of option value due to time decay. It is expressed as a negative value, indicating the loss of option value per day. For example, if the theta value of a call option is -0.02, it means that the option price will decrease by 0.02% per day due to time decay.
### How can I use options Greeks to hedge my positions?
Options Greeks, such as delta, theta, and vega, can be used to hedge positions by offsetting the risk of the underlying stock price movement. For example, if you buy a call option with a delta value of 0.55, you can sell a put option with a delta value of -0.55 to hedge your position. This will reduce the risk of the underlying stock price movement, but it will also reduce the potential profit.
