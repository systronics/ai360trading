## The Numbers That Matter
Compound interest is a powerful force that can help your investments grow exponentially over time. Let's look at some real numbers to illustrate this. Suppose you invest $1,000 in a stock market index fund that earns an average annual return of 7%. After one year, your investment would be worth $1,070. Not bad, but let's see what happens if you leave the investment alone for 10 years. With compound interest, your investment would grow to over $1,967, a return of nearly 97%. Now, imagine if you had invested $100 per month for 10 years, totaling $12,000. With compound interest, your investment would be worth over $23,000, a return of nearly 92%. These numbers are staggering, and they demonstrate the power of compound interest. ## Best Case vs Worst Case
Of course, there are no guarantees in the stock market, and there are risks associated with investing. But the best case scenario is that your investments will grow significantly over time, providing you with a comfortable retirement or achieving your long-term financial goals. The worst case scenario is that the market will decline, and you may lose some or all of your investment.
