## Reader Questions
### FAQ
1. **How to use the RSI indicator correctly for stocks and crypto, including the Nifty, in 2026?**
The RSI indicator can be used correctly by identifying overbought and oversold conditions, as well as RSI divergence. For instance, if the RSI is above 70, it may indicate an overbought condition, while an RSI below 30 may indicate an oversold condition. RSI divergence, on the other hand, occurs when the RSI is moving in the opposite direction of the price. This can be a strong indication of a potential reversal.
2. **What is RSI divergence and how to spot it on the Nifty?**
RSI divergence occurs when the RSI is moving in the opposite direction of the price. For example, if the Nifty is making a new high, but the RSI is not, it may indicate a potential sell signal. On the other hand, if the Nifty is making a new low, but the RSI is not, it may indicate a potential buy signal. Traders can spot RSI divergence by looking for divergences between the RSI and the price.
3. **Does the RSI indicator really work for trading, and what are its limitations?**
The RSI indicator can be a useful tool for trading, but it is not foolproof. It can provide false signals, and it should be used in conjunction with other indicators and analysis. The RSI indicator is also sensitive to the time frame used, and it may not work as well in certain market conditions. Traders should always use the RSI indicator with caution and in combination with other forms of analysis.
