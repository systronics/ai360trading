The dollar index, which measures the value of the US dollar against a basket of major currencies, also plays a crucial role in determining the direction of global markets. The role of crude oil, bond yields, and the Federal Reserve (Fed) cannot be overstated. Bond yields, which reflect the return on investment for government securities, are closely watched by investors as they provide insights into the direction of interest rates. The Fed, as the central bank of the United States, plays a vital role in shaping monetary policy, which has a direct impact on global markets. For instance, when the Fed hikes interest rates, it can lead to a strengthening of the US dollar, making exports from emerging markets more expensive and, consequently, affecting their stock markets.
