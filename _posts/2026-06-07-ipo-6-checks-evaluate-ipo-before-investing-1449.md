## Country By Country View
From a country-by-country perspective, the IPO process differs slightly. In India, investors can apply for an IPO online through the Application Supported by Blocked Amount (ASBA) or Unified Payments Interface (UPI) process. Indian traders can open a free account at [Zerodha](https://zerodha.com/open-account/) to apply for IPOs. In the US, investors can apply for an IPO through a brokerage account, such as [Webull](https://webull.com/). I'd argue that understanding the IPO process in your respective country is crucial to making informed investment decisions.

## Trader FAQs
### What is an IPO, and how do I apply for it?
An IPO is an initial public offering, where a private company issues stock to the public for the first time. To apply for an IPO, you can use the ASBA or UPI process in India or open a brokerage account in the US.
### How do I evaluate an IPO before investing in the share market today?
To evaluate an IPO, consider the company's financials, valuation, promoter quality, and use of funds. Evaluate the company's revenue growth, profit margins, and debt-to-equity ratio to understand its financial health.
### What is IPO GMP, and what are its limits?
IPO GMP refers to the grey market premium, which is the premium at which the IPO's shares are trading in the grey market before listing. However, it's essential to understand that GMP is not a reliable indicator of the IPO's true value, and its limits are often exaggerated.
