## Key Numbers to Know
When it comes to investing in the stock market, there are several key numbers to know. For example, the historical average annual return of the S&P 500 is around 10%. This means that if you invest $1,000 in the S&P 500 index fund, you can potentially earn a 10% return per year, or $100. Over a 10-year period, this can add up to significant gains. Another important number to know is the fees associated with investing in the stock market. For example, the average fee for an actively managed fund is around 1.5%, while the average fee for an index fund is around 0.05%. This can make a significant difference in your returns over time. For instance, if you invest $1,000 in an actively managed fund with a 1.5% fee, you'll pay $15 in fees per year, compared to just $0.50 in fees per year if you invest in an index fund with a 0.05% fee.

## The Risk Nobody's Talking About
One of the biggest risks facing investors in the stock market is the risk of emotional decision-making. When the market is volatile, it can be tempting to make impulsive decisions based on fear or greed. Lekin, this can often lead to poor investment decisions, such as buying high and selling low. To avoid this risk, it's essential to have a long-term perspective and a well-thought-out investment strategy. This can include dollar-cost averaging, where you invest a fixed amount of money at regular intervals, regardless of the market's performance. It can also include diversifying your portfolio, by investing in a range of assets, such as stocks, bonds, and real estate. By having a diversified portfolio, you can potentially reduce your risk and increase your returns over time.
