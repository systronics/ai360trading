## Numbers to Watch
When it comes to investing in the stock market, there are several numbers to watch. The first is the standard deviation of the market, which can help investors understand the level of risk involved. A standard deviation of 15% means that the market can fluctuate by 15% in either direction over a given period. Another important number is the beta correlation, which measures the volatility of a stock or portfolio relative to the overall market. A beta of 1 means that the stock or portfolio is as volatile as the market, while a beta of 2 means that it is twice as volatile. I've seen many investors get caught up in the excitement of high-beta stocks, but it's essential to understand the risks involved.
