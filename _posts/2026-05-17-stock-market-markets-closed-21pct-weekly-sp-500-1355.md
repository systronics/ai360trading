When it comes to investing in the stock market, it's essential to understand the concept of compound interest. Compound interest is the interest earned on both the principal amount and any accrued interest over time. For example, if you invest $1,000 with an annual return of 7%, you'll earn $70 in interest in the first year, making your total balance $1,070. In the second year, you'll earn 7% interest on the new balance of $1,070, resulting in $74.90 in interest, and so on.
