## The Macro Driver Today
The single biggest macro force driving markets today is undoubtedly the surge in crude oil prices, with WTI crude oil jumping 10.89% to $96.75. This sudden move has sent shockwaves across global markets, with the S&P 500 down 1.08% at 6,708.39 and the NIFTY 50 down 0.95% at 23,639.15. The US 10Y Yield has also risen to 4.25, indicating a shift in investor sentiment towards risk-off assets. The tape is telling me that this move in crude oil is not just a one-off event, but rather a sign of a larger trend that could impact markets for the foreseeable future.

## How It's Moving Each Market
The crude oil surge has had a ripple effect on various markets, with the DXY (Dollar) rising 0.45% to 99.68 and the EUR/USD falling 0.86% to 1.15. The Bitcoin price, however, has shown resilience, holding above the $70,000 level at $70,415.88. Ethereum has also seen a slight increase, up 1.24% to $2,077.02. The Crypto Fear & Greed Index is still in the "Extreme Fear" zone at 18, indicating a high level of uncertainty among investors. What I'm watching for is how the crude oil price movement will impact the crypto market in the short term. Historically, such large moves in crude oil have led to increased volatility in other assets, including cryptocurrencies.

The news of Bitcoin and Ethereum ETFs drawing $172M in combined inflows as institutions buy the dip is a significant development. This suggests that smart money is positioning itself for a potential upside move in the crypto market. The SEC and CFTC signing a historic MoU to harmonize crypto oversight is also a positive step towards regulatory clarity. As I see it, the current setup reminds me of August 2023 when the NIFTY bounced hard from exactly the same zone. The level that matters today is the $70,000 support for Bitcoin, which has been tested multiple times in the past few days.
