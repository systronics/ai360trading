## How This Affects Each Country
The IPO landscape varies across countries, with different regulatory frameworks and market conditions. In India, the Securities and Exchange Board of India (SEBI) regulates the IPO process, while in the US, the Securities and Exchange Commission (SEC) oversees the listings. Indian traders can open a free account at [Zerodha](https://zerodha.com/open-account/) to apply for IPOs, while US investors can use platforms like [Webull](https://webull.com/). The IPO process in India involves applying through the Application Supported by Blocked Amount (ASBA) or Unified Payments Interface (UPI) routes. In the US, investors can apply through their brokerage accounts. It's essential to understand the specific requirements and processes for each country to navigate the IPO market effectively. I'd argue that understanding the local market conditions and regulations is vital for investors looking to participate in IPOs.

## Key Numbers to Know
When evaluating IPOs, investors should focus on key numbers such as valuation, revenue growth, and return on equity (ROE). For instance, an IPO with a valuation of 20 times its earnings may be considered expensive, while one with an ROE of 15% may be more attractive. It's also essential to consider the standard deviation of the company's stock price, as well as its beta correlation with the broader market. Volatility clustering, where periods of high volatility are followed by periods of low volatility, is another crucial factor to consider. I've found that investors who focus on these key numbers tend to make more informed decisions. The Relative Strength Index (RSI) and Moving Average Convergence Divergence (MACD) readings can also provide valuable insights into the stock's momentum and trend.

* What is IPO GMP, and what are its real limits? 
IPO GMP, or grey market premium, refers to the premium at which the IPO's unlisted shares are trading in the grey market. However, GMP is a flawed indicator, and investors should not rely solely on it to make their decisions.
