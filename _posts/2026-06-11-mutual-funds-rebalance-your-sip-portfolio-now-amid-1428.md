## The Numbers That Actually Matter
When evaluating a mutual fund, investors should look at the expense ratio, AUM (Assets Under Management), rolling returns, and the fund manager's track record. A lower expense ratio and higher AUM can indicate a more stable and well-managed fund. Rolling returns can give investors an idea of the fund's performance over different time periods. For example, a fund with a 3-year rolling return of 15% per annum may be a better investment than a fund with a 3-year rolling return of 10% per annum.
