What is a step-up SIP?

A step-up SIP (also called top-up SIP) increases your monthly investment by a fixed percentage every year — usually matching your salary increment. Start at ₹5,000/month and step up 10% yearly: year 2 becomes ₹5,500, year 3 ₹6,050, and so on.

The effect is dramatic because the LARGER contributions come in your later years and every increase compounds. A 10% annual step-up can add 40–60% more wealth over 15–20 years compared with a flat SIP — from money you'd barely miss, since your income grows too.

How this calculator works

It simulates every month: your money grows at the monthly rate, your contribution is added, and once every 12 months the contribution increases by your step-up percentage. It then compares against the same SIP without any step-up.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is a step-up SIP better than a normal SIP?
If your income grows every year, almost always yes — you invest more when you can afford more, and the final corpus is far larger. The comparison line above shows exactly how much extra.
Can I set up automatic step-up with my fund house?
Yes — most mutual fund platforms and AMCs offer a "top-up SIP" option where the increase happens automatically every year. You can also do it manually.
