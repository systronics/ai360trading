1. Groww

✅ Easiest for first-timers
Ease of use: ★★★★★

Groww is the app most beginners find simplest. It looks like a clean, friendly app rather than a trader's terminal — you can start a mutual-fund SIP or buy a share in a few taps, with plain-language screens and no clutter. Account opening is free. If the market feels intimidating, start here.

Best for: total beginners, SIP/mutual-fund investors, anyone who wants a no-fuss app.

2. Zerodha (Kite)

✅ Best to grow into
Ease of use: ★★★★☆

Zerodha's Kite app is a little more "market-like", but it's clean, reliable and backed by India's largest broker. It's a great choice if you want an app you won't outgrow — simple enough to start, powerful enough as you learn. Equity delivery is free; a small one-time account fee applies.

Best for: beginners who want a trusted, long-term home for their investments.

3. Dhan

✅ Best if you'll trade actively
Ease of use: ★★★★☆

Dhan is fast and modern, with built-in TradingView charts and quick order placement. It's slightly more feature-rich than a pure beginner app, so it suits you if you already know you'll do more than just invest — intraday or options later. Free account opening and free delivery.

Best for: beginners who plan to become active traders.

How to pick in one line

Want the simplest app? Groww. Want one you'll never outgrow? Zerodha. Plan to trade actively? Dhan. All are free to open, so you can try more than one and keep the app you like. For a deeper look at charges and account types, see our best demat account comparison.

How to start (5 minutes)

  1. Tap "Open app" above and enter your mobile number.
  2. Keep your PAN and Aadhaar (linked to your mobile) ready.
  3. Finish the quick KYC — selfie and signature, on your phone.
  4. E-sign with the Aadhaar OTP.
  5. Add money and start with a small SIP or a single share.

Frequently Asked Questions

Which trading app is easiest for a complete beginner?
Most first-timers find Groww the simplest, thanks to its clean, friendly design and easy mutual-fund SIPs. Zerodha and Dhan are also beginner-friendly with a little more depth.
Are these apps safe?
Yes. All are registered with SEBI and your shares sit with government depositories (CDSL/NSDL). Never share your password or OTP.
Can I use more than one app?
Yes. Since opening is free with most, many people try two and keep the one they prefer.
Do I need money to open the app?
No. Opening is free (Zerodha has a small one-time fee). You only add money when you're ready to invest, and you can start with a small amount.
