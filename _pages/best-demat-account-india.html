1. Zerodha

✅ Best overall / most trusted

Zerodha is India's largest stockbroker by active clients, and the one most long-term investors start with. Its Kite app and web platform are clean and reliable, equity delivery (buy-and-hold) is free, and intraday/F&O carry a low flat per-order charge. If you just want a dependable account to build long-term investments and occasionally trade, this is the safe default.

Good for
  • Long-term investors & beginners who want reliability
  • Free equity delivery (holding shares)
  • Huge user base, lots of tutorials & support content
Keep in mind
  • Small one-time account-opening fee
  • App can slow down on very high-volatility days

2. Dhan

✅ Best for active & options traders

Dhan is a newer, fast-moving platform popular with active traders. It has quick order placement, built-in TradingView charts, and strong tools for intraday and options (F&O) — with free account opening and free equity delivery. If you plan to trade regularly rather than just invest, Dhan's speed and charting are a real advantage.

Good for
  • Intraday & options/F&O traders
  • Free account opening + free delivery
  • TradingView charts & fast order entry built in
Keep in mind
  • Feature-rich — a little much for total beginners
  • Newer brand than Zerodha (still fully SEBI-regulated)

3. Groww

✅ Best for beginners & mutual funds

Groww is the friendliest app for first-timers. It began with mutual funds and now offers stocks, IPOs and more in one simple interface, with free account opening. If words like "F&O" and "margin" feel scary and you mostly want to start SIPs and buy a few shares, Groww is the gentlest place to begin.

How to choose (in one line)

Beginner, mostly investing? Start with Groww or Zerodha. Serious long-term investor? Zerodha. Active trader / options? Dhan. There's no harm in opening more than one free account and keeping the app you like best — most are free to open.

How to open a demat account (5 minutes, 100% online)

  1. Tap "Open account" above and enter your mobile number & email.
  2. Keep your PAN card and Aadhaar (linked to your mobile) ready.
  3. Complete the quick KYC — a selfie and a signature, done on your phone.
  4. E-sign with the Aadhaar OTP. No printing or courier needed.
  5. Account is usually ready in a few hours to a day — then add money and start.

What you need

PAN card, Aadhaar linked to your mobile number, a bank account in your name, and a phone with a camera. That's it — everything is verified digitally.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is it safe to open a demat account online?
Yes. All the brokers above are registered with SEBI and your shares are held with government depositories (CDSL/NSDL), not the broker. Never share your password or OTP with anyone.
Can I open more than one demat account?
Yes, you can have accounts with multiple brokers. Many people keep one for investing and one for trading. Opening is free with most of the above.
Is there any charge to open or hold the account?
Account opening is free with Dhan and Groww and a small one-time fee with Zerodha. Brokers may charge a small yearly maintenance (AMC) — check the current amount on their site.
Do I need a lot of money to start?
No. You can start a mutual-fund SIP with as little as ₹100–₹500 a month, or buy a single share. Start small while you learn.
