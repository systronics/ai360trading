How to calculate percentages

Percent of a number: multiply the number by the percentage and divide by 100. Example: 15% of 200 = (15 × 200) ÷ 100 = 30.

One number as a percent of another: divide the first by the second and multiply by 100. Example: 45 of 180 = (45 ÷ 180) × 100 = 25%.

Percentage change: subtract the old value from the new, divide by the old value, and multiply by 100. A positive result is an increase, a negative one a decrease.

Frequently Asked Questions

How do I find a percentage of a number quickly?
Multiply the number by the percentage and divide by 100. For 20% of 50, that is (20 × 50) ÷ 100 = 10.
How do I calculate percentage increase?
Subtract the original value from the new value, divide by the original, then multiply by 100.
