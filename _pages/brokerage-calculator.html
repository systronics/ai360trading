What eats into your trading profit

Your gross profit is just (sell − buy) × quantity. But your real take-home is lower because of statutory + broker charges:

  • Brokerage — the broker's fee. Discount brokers: ₹0 on delivery, and ₹20 (or 0.03%) per intraday order.
  • STT — Securities Transaction Tax. Delivery 0.1% both sides; intraday 0.025% on sell.
  • Exchange + SEBI — tiny turnover-based charges.
  • GST — 18% on brokerage + exchange charges.
  • Stamp duty — on the buy side; plus a small DP charge when you sell delivery holdings.

For small trades these charges can be larger than a thin profit — which is exactly why the breakeven move figure matters: the price move you need just to cover costs.

Frequently Asked Questions

How is brokerage calculated in India?
Brokerage plus STT, exchange transaction charges, SEBI fee, 18% GST (on brokerage + exchange), stamp duty and DP charges. Discount brokers charge ₹0 on delivery and ₹20 (or 0.03%) per intraday order.
Why is my actual profit less than (sell − buy)?
Because STT, GST, exchange, stamp and DP charges are deducted on top of brokerage. On small trades these can exceed a thin profit.
Is delivery really zero brokerage?
With discount brokers, yes — delivery brokerage is ₹0 (you still pay STT, stamp and a small DP charge). Full-service brokers may charge a percentage.
Are these charges exact?
They are close estimates using standard discount-broker (NSE equity) rates. Exact figures vary slightly by broker, exchange and any rate revisions — check your contract note.
